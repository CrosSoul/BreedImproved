# Breed Improved v0.1.0

## Feature summary

Breed Improved v0.1.0 introduces **Exile from Dynasty**, an individual, player-initiated Character Interaction. A living player-controlled Dynast can confirm the exile of one eligible Dynasty member. The target and their descendants enter a replacement Dynasty generated by CK3.

## Supported CK3 version and languages

- CK3 compatibility metadata: `1.19.*`
- Runtime acceptance environment: CK3 `1.19.0.6`
- Localisation: English and Simplified Chinese

Compatibility with untested CK3 versions is not claimed.

## Eligibility

The target must be a living, highborn, AI-controlled member of the actor's Dynasty. The actor cannot target themself, a House Head, or the Dynast.

Runtime acceptance covers:

- minors and adults;
- unlanded characters and landed rulers; and
- the current player heir.

## Prestige cost

The native Character Interaction cost is `0` personal Prestige when the target:

- has `bastard`;
- has `legitimized_bastard`; or
- has at least one explicitly existing, script-visible legal parent outside the actor's Dynasty.

All other eligible targets cost `100` personal Prestige. Breed Improved does not manually subtract Prestige.

Missing parents do not qualify by themselves. Only legal parent scopes visible to script are checked. Spouses, descendants, hidden biological-parent secrets, and ancestry beyond parents are not part of the cost rule.

## Gameplay consequences

On acceptance:

- the target and descendants enter a generated replacement Dynasty;
- parents and siblings remain unchanged;
- an existing `house_head_hook` held by the actor over the target is removed;
- the target receives `-60` opinion of the actor with monthly recovery of `0.5`;
- the target gains `60` base stress;
- the target receives a ten-year visible modifier containing:
  - `diplomacy = -1`;
  - `stress_gain_mult = 0.2`;
  - `monthly_prestige = -0.25`; and
- the target receives a permanent internal exile marker.

The interaction does not directly alter claims, marriage, titles, court membership, government, imprisonment, or other political status. CK3 may recalculate succession and the player heir when Dynasty affiliation changes.

## Distribution and installation

Steam Workshop is the sole supported normal installation channel:

1. Open the **Breed Improved** Workshop item: https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3769010534 and subscribe when it is available to you.
2. Enable the Mod in a CK3 Launcher playset.
3. Launch the game.

The Workshop version is the official playable release. GitHub is the public source, history, issue, roadmap, documentation, tag, and Release-notes channel; it does not provide a supported manual-install package. GitHub-generated source archives must not be described as installable Mod packages.

## Removal

Disable the Mod in the CK3 Launcher and unsubscribe through Steam Workshop. Back up saves before changing a playset. No automatic rollback is provided for consequences already stored in a save.

## Known limitations and behavior

- Replacement Dynasty names and coats of arms are generated by CK3.
- Lowborn and player-controlled targets are excluded.
- House Heads and the Dynast are excluded.
- A married target may later receive claims through normal CK3 mechanics. The cause of the deferred spouse-related strong-claim observation has not been established.
- Compatibility with other mods has not been comprehensively tested.
- Bulk Dynasty cleanup is not included in v0.1.0.

## Explicitly excluded from v0.1.0

- automatic or scheduled scanning;
- background or recurring execution;
- bulk cleanup;
- Decisions or event chains;
- an accepted-founder-parent whitelist;
- ancestry checks beyond legal parents;
- divorce or claim cleanup.

Every operation requires player initiation and confirmation.

## Deferred future work
